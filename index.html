Thesis

A Solvable Model of Generative Diffusion and the Memorization Phenomenon

MSc thesis: Bocconi University · Supervisor: C. Lucibello

This thesis studies why learning the empirical score would imply memorization, and how real diffusion systems avoid this degeneracy through architectural constraints and optimization dynamics.

Thesis (PDF) Talk slides (PDF)
Abstract

This thesis investigates the mechanisms underlying memorization and generalization in score-based diffusion models. If the empirical score, namely the empirical loss minimizer, was used during generation, diffusion models would merely reproduce training samples. Yet, state-of-the-art systems consistently avoid this degeneracy, generating novel and coherent data. Understanding this apparent paradox requires analyzing what the learned score truly represents, and how architectural and dynamical constraints shape it. Building on recently developed theoretical frameworks, we study these questions within the analytically tractable setting of Random Feature Neural Networks (RFNNs). Through both analytical derivations and numerical experiments, we show that generalization emerges from the interplay between architecture and optimization dynamics. The ratio of parameters to data controls an architectural phase transition between under- and over-parameterized regimes, while the training trajectory itself induces implicit dynamical regularization, separating fast generalization from slow memorization timescales. We then extend this framework in two directions. First, we introduce time-integrated RFNNs with bias and skip connections, bridging the gap between solvable models and realistic diffusion architectures, and revealing how temporal conditioning modulates the balance between generalization and memorization. Second, we analyze structured data through Gaussian mixtures, uncovering a Baik-Ben Arous-Péché (BBP) transition that marks the spectral onset of signal detectability. Together, these results support a unified picture in which diffusion models generalize not by perfectly reproducing empirical scores, but by dynamically and imperfectly learning smooth score fields shaped by architecture, data, and optimization dynamics.
